About Hinduism

Hinduism is one of the world's oldest religions, deeply rooted in spiritual traditions, scriptures, and cultural heritage. It is a way of life that emphasizes Dharma (righteousness), Karma (actions and their consequences), and Moksha (liberation).

Core Concepts

  • Sanatan Dharma: The eternal duty that guides Hindu beliefs and practices.
  • Vedas and Upanishads: The sacred scriptures that contain spiritual knowledge and wisdom.
  • Bhakti, Jnana, Karma, and Raja Yoga: The four main paths to spiritual realization.
  • Deities: Hindus worship various forms of the Divine, including Brahma, Vishnu, Shiva, Lakshmi, Saraswati, and more.
  • Festivals: Celebrations like Diwali, Holi, Navaratri, and Janmashtami hold immense cultural and spiritual significance.
